PB Banana Baked Oats

Thick, cake-like baked oats packed with peanut butter, banana and protein powder. Make the full week's batch in the airfryer Sunday — just microwave each morning. Tastes like a warm peanut butter muffin.

💪 ~35g protein ⏱ 90 sec microwave 💰 ~$1.80 ✈️ Airfryer (Sunday batch)

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Rolled oats80g (1 cup)
  • Milk120ml
  • Peanut butter2 tbsp
  • Protein powder (vanilla)1 scoop (30g)
  • Banana½, mashed
  • Honey1 tsp
  • Pinch of salttiny pinch
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (Sunday — make 3 serves at once)

  1. Mix the batter: In a bowl, mash 1.5 bananas well. Add 240g oats, 360ml milk, 6 tbsp peanut butter, 3 scoops protein powder, 3 tsp honey and a pinch of salt. Stir until fully combined — it'll be thick like porridge.
  2. Portion into dishes: Divide evenly into 3 small oven-safe ramekins or foil tins that fit your airfryer. Fill each about ¾ full — they puff up slightly.
  3. Airfryer bake: Place in airfryer at 160°C for 18–20 minutes until the top is set and slightly golden. A toothpick in the centre should come out mostly clean. Let cool, then lid/wrap and refrigerate.
  4. Morning reheat: Microwave 90 seconds. Top with banana slices and drizzle of honey. Eat straight from the ramekin.
💡 Tip: Small foil baking trays from Coles/Woolies (~$3 for 10) are perfect here — they go straight in the airfryer and you don't need to wash anything.

Airfryer Chicken Rice Bowl

Juicy garlic-seasoned airfryer chicken breast sliced over steamed jasmine rice with soy sauce, cucumber and carrot. The foundation of the whole week's lunches — nail the chicken Sunday and you're set.

💪 ~45g protein ⏱ 3 min microwave 💰 ~$4.20 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Chicken breast180g
  • Jasmine rice (dry)80g (¾ cup)
  • Soy sauce1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder½ tsp
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Salt & pepperto taste
  • Cucumber¼, sliced
  • Carrot½, grated
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (Sunday — make 4 serves of chicken)

  1. Prep chicken: Pat 4 chicken breasts dry with paper towel. This is important — dry chicken crisps up, wet chicken steams. Rub each one with olive oil, garlic powder, salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Airfryer setup: Preheat airfryer to 200°C for 3 minutes. Don't skip this — a cold airfryer gives uneven results.
  3. Cook chicken: Place breasts in airfryer basket without overlapping. Cook at 200°C for 18 minutes, flipping at the 9-minute mark. Thicker breasts may need 20 minutes — slice into the thickest part to check it's white all the way through, no pink.
  4. Rest the chicken: Remove and let rest on a board for 5 minutes before slicing. This keeps the juices inside. Slice into thick strips.
  5. Cook rice: While chicken rests, rinse 400g dry rice until water runs clear. Add to pot with 640ml cold water. Bring to boil, then put lid on, turn to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes. Remove from heat, leave lid on 5 more minutes. Fluff with fork.
  6. Portion bowls: Divide rice into 4 containers. Add sliced chicken on top. Veg goes in a separate small bag or section so it stays crisp. Pack soy sauce in a tiny container on the side.
  7. To eat: Microwave rice + chicken 3 minutes. Add raw cucumber and carrot on top, drizzle soy sauce. Done.
💡 Tip: If your airfryer runs hot, check at 15 minutes. Every airfryer is different — use a meat thermometer if you have one (internal temp should reach 75°C).

Airfryer Bolognese Pasta Bake

Beef mince with canned tomatoes, garlic and Italian herbs tossed through penne and topped with parmesan — all baked in the airfryer until bubbling. Sunday prep, Monday reheat. Pure comfort.

💪 ~42g protein ⏱ 5 min reheat 💰 ~$5.00 ✈️ Airfryer (Sunday batch)

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Beef mince180g
  • Penne pasta (dry)90g
  • Canned diced tomatoes½ can (200g)
  • Tomato paste1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder1 tsp
  • Italian herbs (dried)1 tsp
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Salt & pepperto taste
  • Parmesan (optional)small sprinkle
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (Sunday — makes 2 serves)

  1. Cook pasta first: Boil penne in a pot of salted water for 10 minutes until just tender. Drain, toss with a tiny bit of olive oil so it doesn't stick. Set aside. (This is the only water boiling in the whole week — pasta needs it.)
  2. Brown mince in airfryer: Break beef mince into a foil tray or airfryer-safe dish. Drizzle with olive oil, add garlic powder, salt and pepper. Cook at 200°C for 10 minutes, breaking up with a fork halfway through, until fully browned with no pink.
  3. Add sauce: Remove tray from airfryer. Stir in diced tomatoes, tomato paste and Italian herbs directly into the mince. Mix well.
  4. Add pasta: Add cooked pasta to the tray and stir everything together until the pasta is coated in sauce.
  5. Bake: Return tray to airfryer at 180°C for 8 more minutes until sauce is bubbling and slightly thickened. Sprinkle parmesan on in the last 2 minutes if using.
  6. Portion and store: Divide into 2 containers. Refrigerate. Monday: microwave 4 minutes, stir, eat.
💡 Tip: Yes, pasta still needs boiling water — there's no way around that. But the mince and sauce cook entirely in the airfryer, so it's still mostly one appliance.

Berry Protein Smoothie

Frozen mixed berries, banana, peanut butter, honey and protein powder blended with milk. Pre-bag everything Sunday night — Tuesday morning is literally 45 seconds of blending.

💪 ~38g protein ⏱ 45 sec blend 💰 ~$2.00 🥤 Blender

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Frozen mixed berries1 cup (150g)
  • Banana1 medium
  • Peanut butter1 tbsp
  • Protein powder (vanilla)1 scoop (30g)
  • Honey1 tsp
  • Milk300ml
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

  1. Sunday night prep: Put frozen berries, banana (peeled and broken in half), and peanut butter into a ziplock bag. Seal and put back in freezer. Put protein powder + honey in a separate small container.
  2. Tuesday morning: Dump the frozen bag contents into the blender. Add milk. Add protein powder and honey.
  3. Blend: Blend on high for 45–60 seconds until completely smooth. If it's too thick, add a splash more milk.
  4. Drink: Pour into a big cup or drink straight from the blender. Done — no dishes except the blender cup.
💡 Tip: Frozen berries make it thick and cold — no need for ice. If you don't have a blender, mash everything in a bowl and eat as a smoothie bowl with a spoon. Still delicious.

Chicken & Hummus Wrap

Sunday airfryer chicken sliced into strips, loaded into a flatbread with hummus, roasted capsicum and baby spinach. Roll it up, wrap in foil, microwave before school.

💪 ~40g protein ⏱ 90 sec microwave 💰 ~$3.80 🌯 Assemble

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Mountain bread / flatbread1 large
  • Airfryer chicken (from Sunday)150g sliced
  • Hummus3 tbsp
  • Roasted capsicum (jar)3–4 strips
  • Baby spinachhandful
  • Salt & pepperto taste
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

  1. Lay flatbread flat: Place your flatbread on a sheet of foil roughly the same size.
  2. Spread hummus: Spread 3 tablespoons of hummus evenly across the centre of the wrap, leaving 3cm clear on each edge.
  3. Add fillings: Layer spinach first (it protects the bread from getting soggy). Then add sliced chicken strips. Then roasted capsicum strips on top. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roll tightly: Fold in the sides first (2cm each), then roll from the bottom upward, keeping it tight. Don't overfill or it'll split.
  5. Wrap in foil: Wrap the whole thing snugly in the foil. Store in fridge overnight or prep morning-of.
  6. Reheat: Microwave in foil 90 seconds. The steam inside the foil heats it through evenly. Squeeze to check it's hot in the centre.
💡 Tip: Don't add tomato or cucumber inside — they go soggy overnight. Pack fresh veg separately if you want it.

Kotlety with Airfryer Potato Wedges

Juicy Russian beef patties cooked golden in the airfryer, served with crispy seasoned potato wedges — also airfryer. Both cook simultaneously at the same temperature. Shape the patties Sunday, cook fresh Tuesday in 20 minutes.

💪 ~46g protein ⏱ 20 min airfryer 💰 ~$5.50 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (2 serves)

  • Beef mince350g
  • Breadcrumbs3 tbsp
  • Milk2 tbsp
  • Brown onion½, grated
  • Garlic powder1 tsp
  • Salt¾ tsp
  • Black pepper½ tsp
  • Potatoes2 medium, wedged
  • Olive oil1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder (wedges)½ tsp
  • Sour cream (to serve)1 tbsp
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

  1. Sunday — shape kotlety: Combine beef mince, breadcrumbs, milk, grated onion, garlic powder, salt and pepper in a bowl. Mix with your hands 2 minutes. Wet hands, shape into 6 oval patties about 10cm long and 2cm thick. Place on a lined tray, cover and refrigerate.
  2. Tuesday — prep wedges: Cut potatoes into wedges (8 per potato). Toss in a bowl with olive oil, garlic powder and salt until evenly coated.
  3. Start wedges first: Add wedges to airfryer basket at 200°C for 20 minutes total, shaking at 10 minutes.
  4. Add kotlety at the 8-minute mark: When wedges have been going 8 minutes, add kotlety patties alongside (or on the rack above if your airfryer has one). Cook remaining 12 minutes together, flipping patties at the halfway point.
  5. Check done: Kotlety should be golden brown outside and cooked through — slice one to check. Wedges should be crispy and golden.
  6. Serve: Plate together with a dollop of sour cream on the side.
💡 Tip: Grate the onion (not dice) — it distributes through every bite and keeps the patties moist. The milk-soaked breadcrumbs are the Russian secret to juicy kotlety.

Airfryer Mexican Beef Bowl

Mexican-spiced beef mince cooked in the airfryer over jasmine rice with corn, diced tomato and sour cream. Bold and filling — the mince goes in the airfryer Sunday in a foil tray, no stirring needed.

💪 ~44g protein ⏱ 3 min microwave 💰 ~$4.20 ✈️ Airfryer (Sunday batch)

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Beef mince180g
  • Taco seasoning1 tbsp
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Water2 tbsp
  • Cooked jasmine rice150g (Sunday batch)
  • Canned corn kernels3 tbsp, drained
  • Diced tomato½ tomato
  • Sour cream1 tbsp
  • Lime juicesqueeze
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (Sunday prep)

Airfryer Garlic Beef & Rice

Juicy beef strips marinated in garlic, soy and a touch of tomato paste, cooked in the airfryer until caramelised. Served over rice with roasted capsicum and a dollop of sour cream. Bold, rich and dead easy.

💪 ~44g protein ⏱ 10 min airfryer 💰 ~$5.00 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Beef stir fry strips200g
  • Soy sauce1 tbsp
  • Tomato paste1 tsp
  • Garlic powder1 tsp
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Cooked jasmine rice150g (from Sunday)
  • Roasted capsicum (jar)3 strips
  • Sour cream1 tbsp
  • Salt & pepperto taste
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

  1. Marinate beef: In a bowl, mix beef strips with soy sauce, tomato paste, garlic powder and olive oil. Toss well to coat every strip. If you have 10 minutes to let it sit, even better — but not required.
  2. Preheat airfryer: Set airfryer to 200°C for 3 minutes.
  3. Cook beef: Spread beef strips in a single layer in the basket — don't pile them or they'll steam instead of caramelise. Cook at 200°C for 8–10 minutes, shaking the basket at the 5-minute mark. They should be dark and slightly crispy on the edges.
  4. Reheat rice: While beef cooks, microwave your Sunday rice 2 minutes until steaming hot.
  5. Plate up: Spoon rice into a bowl. Lay beef strips over the top. Add roasted capsicum strips on the side. Finish with a dollop of sour cream and crack of black pepper.
💡 Tip: The key is a single layer in the airfryer — crowded beef steams and goes grey. If your airfryer is small, cook in two batches. The caramelised edges from the soy + tomato paste are what make this dish.

Pesto Chicken Pasta

Sunday airfryer chicken tossed through penne with jarred basil pesto, cherry tomatoes and parmesan. Works hot or cold — throw it together Sunday night and it's done.

💪 ~43g protein ⏱ 2 min microwave or cold 💰 ~$4.00 🌿 Assemble

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Cooked penne pasta120g
  • Airfryer chicken (Sunday)150g, sliced
  • Basil pesto (jar)2 tbsp
  • Cherry tomatoes6–8, halved
  • Parmesan1 tbsp grated
  • Black pepperto taste
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (Sunday assemble)

  1. Use Sunday pasta: Take your portion of cooked Sunday pasta. Make sure it had a tiny bit of olive oil tossed through when it was drained — this stops it clumping.
  2. Mix pesto through: Add 2 tablespoons of pesto to the pasta and toss well until every piece is coated. Pesto is oil-based so it coats easily.
  3. Add chicken and tomatoes: Lay sliced chicken on top. Add halved cherry tomatoes. Sprinkle parmesan and crack of black pepper.
  4. Seal container: Put lid on and refrigerate. This actually gets better overnight as the pesto soaks into the pasta.
  5. To eat: Eat cold (works perfectly), or microwave 2 minutes with lid slightly open. Stir before eating.
💡 Tip: Pesto pasta is one of the few meals that's genuinely better cold — the flavours develop overnight. Try it cold before microwaving.

Airfryer Beef Stroganoff with Wedges

Tender beef strips in a rich garlic-tomato-sour cream sauce, cooked entirely in the airfryer in a foil tray. Served with crispy airfryer potato wedges instead of mash. The most iconic Russian comfort meal — done in 20 minutes, no stovetop needed.

💪 ~46g protein ⏱ 20 min airfryer 💰 ~$5.50 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Beef stir fry strips200g
  • Sour cream2 tbsp
  • Tomato paste1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder½ tsp
  • Beef stock3 tbsp
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Salt & pepperto taste
  • Potatoes2 medium, wedged
  • Olive oil (wedges)1 tbsp
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (fresh cook Thursday)

  1. Prep wedges: Cut potatoes into wedges. Toss with olive oil, garlic powder and salt. Place in airfryer basket at 200°C — they need 20 minutes total, so start them first.
  2. Make stroganoff sauce: In a small foil tray, mix together tomato paste, sour cream, beef stock, garlic powder, olive oil, salt and pepper until smooth and combined.
  3. Add beef: Add beef strips to the sauce tray and toss to coat every strip fully in the sauce.
  4. Cook beef alongside wedges: At the 8-minute mark (when wedges have 12 minutes left), place the beef tray in the airfryer alongside the wedges. Cook 12 minutes, stirring beef at the halfway point.
  5. Check and serve: Wedges should be golden and crispy. Beef should be cooked through with sauce thickened and caramelised around the edges. Plate together immediately.
💡 Tip: Keep the sour cream to 2 tablespoons — mostly tomato paste with a touch of sour cream keeps it lactose-friendly and stops the sauce splitting in the airfryer heat.

Cheesy Beef Mince Pasta Bake

Friday night comfort food — seasoned beef mince tossed through penne in a rich tomato-garlic sauce, topped with melted cheddar. Everything uses Sunday batch ingredients. Just assemble, airfryer 8 minutes, done.

💪 ~44g protein ⏱ 8 min airfryer 💰 ~$4.50 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Cooked penne (Sunday batch)120g
  • Cooked beef mince (Sunday batch)150g
  • Diced tomatoes (canned)½ can
  • Tomato paste1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder½ tsp
  • Italian herbs½ tsp
  • Cheddar cheese (grated)30g
  • Salt & pepperto taste
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

Beef Bolognese Pasta

Rich, garlicky beef mince simmered with canned tomatoes and Italian herbs. Served over penne with a sprinkle of parmesan. The most classic weeknight dinner — done in one pot.

  • Penne pasta90g dry
  • Canned diced tomatoes½ can (200g)

  • Brown onion½, diced

Kotlety — Airfryer Russian Meat Patties

Juicy Russian-style beef patties seasoned with garlic, onion and herbs, cooked golden in the airfryer in 12 minutes. Served with creamy mashed potato. This is the Russian classic — shaped Sunday, cooked fresh Tuesday.

💪 ~46g protein ⏱ 12 min airfryer 💰 ~$5.50 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (2 serves)

  • Beef mince350g
  • Breadcrumbs3 tbsp
  • Milk2 tbsp
  • Brown onion½, grated
  • Garlic powder1 tsp
  • Salt¾ tsp
  • Black pepper½ tsp
  • Potatoes3 medium
  • Butter1 tbsp
  • Milk (for mash)splash
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method

  1. Sunday — make the mix: Combine beef mince, breadcrumbs, milk, grated onion, garlic powder, salt and pepper in a bowl. Mix well with your hands for 1–2 minutes — this makes them hold together and stay juicy. The milk-soaked breadcrumbs are the secret to Russian kotlety being moist, not dry.
  2. Shape patties: Wet your hands slightly. Shape into oval patties about 10cm long and 2cm thick — like a fat oval burger. Makes about 6 patties. Place on a lined tray, cover with cling wrap and refrigerate.
  3. Tuesday — preheat airfryer: Preheat airfryer to 200°C for 3 minutes. Spray basket lightly with oil.
  4. Cook kotlety: Add patties to basket in a single layer. Cook 12 minutes, flipping at 6 minutes. They should be golden brown outside and fully cooked through — slice one to check if needed.
  5. Mash simultaneously: While airfryer runs, peel and cube potatoes. Boil in salted water 12 minutes until fork-tender. Drain fully. Mash with butter and a splash of milk until smooth. Season with salt.
  6. Serve: Plate kotlety alongside mash. Add a spoonful of sour cream on the side if you like — totally traditional.
💡 Tip: Grating the onion (not dicing) distributes it through every bite and keeps patties moist. If you dice it, you get big chunks. Grated is the Russian way.

Beef Stroganoff with Mash

Tender beef strips in a rich garlicky tomato-sour cream sauce served over creamy mashed potato. The most iconic Russian comfort meal — cooked fresh in 15 minutes on Thursday.

💪 ~46g protein ⏱ 15 min fresh cook 💰 ~$5.50 ✈️ Airfryer

Ingredients (1 serve)

  • Beef stir fry strips200g
  • Sour cream2 tbsp
  • Tomato paste1 tbsp
  • Garlic powder½ tsp
  • Beef stock (or water)100ml
  • Olive oil1 tsp
  • Potatoes2 medium
  • Butter1 tbsp
  • Milksplash
  • Salt & pepperto taste
🛒 Compare prices: Coles ↗ Woolworths ↗ ALDI ↗

Method (fresh cook Thursday)

  1. Boil potatoes first: Peel and cube potatoes (roughly 3cm pieces). Put in pot of cold salted water. Bring to boil and cook 12 minutes while you make the stroganoff — they time out perfectly.
  2. Sear beef strips: Heat frypan on high — it needs to be really hot. Add a tiny bit of oil. Add beef strips in a single layer, don't crowd them. Let them sit undisturbed for 90 seconds to get a brown crust. Flip and cook 60 more seconds. Remove and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce: Turn heat to medium. In same pan, add tomato paste and garlic powder. Stir 30 seconds until it darkens slightly. Add stock and stir well, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom — that's flavour.
  4. Add sour cream: Turn heat to low. Add sour cream and stir until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Don't boil after adding sour cream or it may split — keep it on low.
  5. Combine: Add beef strips back into the sauce. Stir gently, cook 2 more minutes on low. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Mash potatoes: Drain potatoes fully. Mash with butter and a splash of milk until smooth. Season generously with salt.
  7. Plate: Spoon mash into a bowl, ladle stroganoff sauce and beef over the top. Eat immediately.
💡 Tip: The key to stroganoff is searing beef fast on very high heat — don't cook it through, just brown the outside. It finishes gently in the sauce and stays tender, not chewy.
